About the Chettira Family

The Chettira Family is a respected Kodava family originating from Karnangeri village in Madikeri Taluk, Kodagu District, Karnataka. For generations, the family has been closely associated with agriculture, land stewardship, and community service.

This archive is maintained to preserve the family’s heritage, lineage, and historical records for future generations.

Family History

The roots of the Chettira Family trace back several generations in Kodagu, where the ancestors were granted land in recognition of their service and loyalty. These lands formed the foundation of the family’s social and economic standing.

In the early 20th century, the family expanded into multiple branches following traditional inheritance practices. Around the 1920s, an oral partition divided ancestral properties among three main lineages, leading to independent households.

Over time, portions of land were sold, redistributed, and reorganized to meet changing family and economic needs.

Today, the Chettira Family continues to uphold its traditions, unity, and values while adapting to modern education, professions, and lifestyles.
